❓ How to Play

Welcome to Festival Frenzy!

You're a festival promoter competing to build the best festival over 4 rounds. Book artists, place amenities, and manage your reputation to score the most Victory Points (VP).

The Board

Your festival takes place on a hex grid of green fields. You'll place stages (where artists perform), amenities (campsites, security, catering, portaloos), and manage your layout to complete objectives.

Quick Summary

Each round: Take turns booking artists and placing amenities. At the end of each round, events happen and ticket sales are calculated.

Between rounds: Move amenities, optionally open a new stage (costs 15 tickets), and pay upkeep.

Turns per round: Round 1 = 6 turns, Round 2 = 8, Round 3 = 10, Round 4 = 12.

Controls: Click to select, Space to confirm, Tab to sign an artist to hand, Escape to go back.

Game Setup

Each player sets up in order:

1. Place your stage — Click a hex to preview a 3-hex arch shape. Press Space to confirm. This is where your artists will perform. The grey area around the stage is unplayable.

2. Draft your starting hand — You're shown 2 cheap artists (pick 1), 2 expensive artists (pick 1), and 4 amenities (pick 1). Try to pick artists that share amenity costs!

3. Place your starting amenity — Click a hex to preview, then press Space to lock it in. Pick one your cheapest artist needs.

Stages

You start with 1 stage and can open up to 3. Each stage holds 3 artists. Each stage gives +1 Fame. Opening stage 2 or 3 costs 15 tickets during the betwixt phase.

Your Turn

Each turn you choose one action:

Book or Sign an Artist — Click an artist from the Available Artists tab to select them. Press Space to book them onto a stage, or press Tab to sign them to your hand for later.

Place an Amenity — Pick a die from the Dice tab, then click a hex to place the amenity. Amenities stay on the board permanently (unless removed during upkeep).

Press Escape at any time to undo and change your mind.

Betwixt Rounds

Between rounds, each player can:

Move amenities — Rearrange your amenities to better positions.

Open a new stage — Pay 15 tickets to place an additional stage (max 3).

Upkeep

After ticket sales, you pay to maintain your festival. All amenities cost 5 tickets each. Extra stages (2nd and 3rd) cost 10 tickets each. You can choose to discard amenities you can't afford.

🎤 Artists

There are 60 artists across 4 genres: Pop, Rock, Hip Hop, and Electronic. Each artist has:

Fame requirement — Your fame level must be high enough to book them (★0 to ★5).

Amenity costs — Campsites (⛺), security (💂), catering (🚐), and portaloos (🚽) needed. These are not consumed — the amenities just need to exist on your board.

VP — Victory points awarded when booked.

Tickets — Ticket sales the artist generates.

Effect — Special abilities that trigger when booked.

Booking vs Signing

Booking places an artist on a stage immediately. You must meet their fame and amenity requirements.

Signing adds them to your hand. No requirements needed yet — but unplayed hand cards cost VP at game end!

Lineups & Headliners

Each stage holds 3 artists. The 3rd artist is the Headliner — their effect is doubled!

Completing your first lineup each round gives +1 Fame. Your second lineup lets you choose a bonus reward.

Special Guests

If a stage is 1 artist short at round end, you may draw a Special Guest from the deck. If you can afford them, they fill the slot for free (no ticket sales, but their effect still applies).

⛺💂🚐🚽 Amenities

Amenities are placed on any hex that isn't a stage or unplayable area. They serve two purposes: paying for artists and completing objectives.

⛺ Campsites — Generate 5 tickets every round. Essential for income!

💂 Security — Let you avoid negative events (1 security = 1 event blocked). Also needed by many artists and objectives.

🚐 Catering Vans — Required by many artists as booking costs.

🚽 Portaloos — Required by many artists as booking costs.

Key Rules

Amenities don't disappear when used to pay for artists — they stay on the board.

Each amenity costs 5 tickets upkeep per round. You can discard ones you can't afford.

You get amenities from the dice — 5 dice are rolled each round. Some dice let you choose between two types.

Where you place amenities matters! Many council objectives depend on amenity positions relative to stages, edges, and other amenities.

🎯 Objectives

There are three types of objectives that help you earn bonuses throughout the game.

🏛️ Council Objectives

Each player gets a council objective at the start. These challenge you to arrange amenities in specific ways (e.g. "security next to stage", "no portaloos on the edge").

When achieved, you gain a passive benefit (Fame, VP, or bonus tickets) that lasts as long as you meet the requirement.

Warning: If you stop meeting the requirement, the benefit is revoked until you fix it! Complete all your objectives to receive a new one next round.

🎵 Artist Objectives

These are lineup-related challenges (e.g. "3 artists of the same genre on one stage"). They award VP for each stage that meets the condition. They reset every round — you can earn them repeatedly!

🌐 Global Objectives

3 global objectives appear each round. They're first-come, first-served — the first player to complete one claims the reward (Fame, free amenity, VP, artist draw, or extra artist objective).

⚡✨ Events

At the start of each round, 3 global events are drawn face-down. You can see whether each is positive or negative, but not what it does.

How Events Work

At the end of the round, events are revealed for each player. You also draw personal events (1 per stage you own).

Before events are revealed, you can choose to prevent negative events using security. Each security amenity blocks 1 negative event. Security is not consumed — it stays on your board.

Event Effects

Positive events give bonuses like extra tickets, VP, or Fame.

Negative events can cost you tickets, VP, Fame, or even remove amenities. Plan ahead with security!

🏆 How to Win

The player with the most Victory Points (VP) after 4 rounds wins!

VP Sources

Artists — Each booked artist awards their VP value.

Ticket Sales — The player with the most adjusted tickets each round earns +1 VP. Tickets are multiplied by your Fame level:

★0 = ×0.5 · ★1 = ×0.75 · ★2 = ×1 · ★3 = ×1.5 · ★4 = ×2 · ★5 = ×2.5

Upkeep Leftovers — After paying upkeep, gain +1 VP per 10 tickets remaining.

Objectives — Council objectives give passive bonuses. Artist objectives award VP each round. Global objectives give one-time rewards.

Artist Effects — Some headliner effects grant VP directly.

VP Penalties

Hand Penalty — At game end, unplayed artists in your hand have their VP deducted from your total.

Negative Tickets — Ending a round with negative adjusted tickets costs you 3 VP.

Tiebreaker

If VP is tied, the player with the highest total ticket sales across all rounds wins.
